<p><p>The Aircraft Warning Lights market comprises three main types: low-intensity, medium-intensity, and high-intensity warning lights. Low-intensity lights are used for structures under 45 meters, providing visibility without excessive glare. Medium-intensity lights are suitable for taller structures (between 45 and 150 meters) and offer improved visibility at greater distances. High-intensity lights are designed for tall structures exceeding 150 meters, ensuring maximum visibility in varied weather conditions. Each type serves a critical role in aviation safety by alerting pilots to potential obstacles.</p></p>
