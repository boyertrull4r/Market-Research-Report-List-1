<p><p>Brake caliper kits are crucial components in automotive braking systems, designed to house the brake pads and apply pressure on the brake rotors. The market for these kits includes three primary materials: plastic, iron, and aluminum. Plastic calipers are lightweight and resistant to corrosion but may compromise strength under high temperatures. Iron calipers offer durability and heat resistance, making them suitable for heavy-duty applications. Aluminum calipers are favored for their lightweight properties and excellent heat dissipation, enhancing overall performance.</p></p>
