<p><p>The laboratory reactors market comprises two main types: fixed and modular reactors. Fixed reactors are designed for specific processes and production volumes, providing stability and consistent results but offering limited flexibility for changes in production. In contrast, modular reactors are versatile and can be easily reconfigured or expanded to accommodate varying demands and different experiments. This adaptability makes modular reactors suitable for research environments, where requirements can change frequently, leading to increased efficiency and innovation in laboratory settings.</p></p>
