<p><p>The Transcatheter Aortic Valve Replacement (TAVR) market is categorized primarily into three types based on the approach used for the procedure. The transfemoral approach involves accessing the heart through the femoral artery in the leg, allowing for a less invasive procedure with quicker recovery. The transapical approach involves direct access to the heart via the apex, suitable for patients with unsuitable femoral access. "Others" encompasses alternative methods such as transaortic or transvenous approaches, catering to diverse patient anatomies and conditions.</p></p>
