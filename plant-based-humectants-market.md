<p><p>The plant-based humectants market comprises several key types. Sugar alcohols, such as sorbitol and xylitol, attract moisture while providing sweetness and reduced calories. Glycerol, a versatile humectant, retains moisture and enhances texture. Alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs) offer exfoliating properties and hydration. Polysaccharides, like xanthan gum, provide thickening and stabilizing functions. Glycols, including propylene glycol, efficiently draw moisture into products. Other humectants may include fruit extracts and seaweed derivatives, broadening the applications in cosmetics, food, and pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
