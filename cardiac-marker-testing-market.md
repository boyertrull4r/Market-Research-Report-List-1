<p><p>Cardiac marker testing encompasses various methodologies to detect heart-related biomarkers in clinical diagnostics. Chemiluminescence employs light emission for sensitive detection, while immunofluorescence utilizes fluorescent labeling to visualize antigens. ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay) is a widely used technique for quantifying proteins through enzyme reactions. Immunochromatography offers rapid, point-of-care testing with visual results. Other methods may include mass spectrometry and molecular techniques, catering to diverse clinical needs and enhancing the accuracy and speed of cardiac dysfunction diagnosis.</p></p>
